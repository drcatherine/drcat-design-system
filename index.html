What this is, and what I'm hoping you'll poke at

This page is the locked web/product design system, rendered in its own aesthetic so you can judge it as a living thing rather than a spec doc. I'm locking this in to make product + landing pages fast and consistent for the launch. Your eye on it before I build at scale would save me a lot of rework.

  • Palette & contrast — does the blush-ground / black-type / taupe-rollover combination hold up, and is the accent rationing right?
  • Type pairing & scale — the serif/sans split, the sizes, the leading.
  • Component treatment — buttons, cards, badges, the section-header pattern.
  • Anything that reads generic or "AI-template" — that's the thing I most want caught.
On the type — you're seeing the real thing. Every serif headline on this page is Exposure @ EXPO 0 (205TF, Federico Parra Barrios), self-hosted from our licensed Web font — exactly what ships on the live site. Body is Hanken Grotesk. So judge the type as final: the high-contrast serif character, the weight, the pairing against the sans.
North star

Five moves that define the look

The warmth lives in the ground; the black type is the editorial backbone. Everything below is in service of that.

1

High-contrast serif display

Exposure at EXPO 0 — the "classy" weight — paired with a humanist sans body. Mixed case, never blanket uppercase.

2

Black CTAs, taupe rollover

Soft-rect 10px, white label. Black is the action; hover rolls to taupe. That's the whole interaction story.

3

One warm temperature

Blush ground, black type, a deeper-blush pop. No cold grays anywhere, top to bottom.

4

Mobile-first commerce

Above-the-fold clarity and a sticky CTA on selling surfaces. Editorial brands under-convert without it.

5

Art direction carries richness

Warm editorial photography of real women thriving + Jordan Price's illustrated botanicals. The UI stays quiet.

Color

A warm canvas, one black workhorse, a single pop

Stark black does type, headings, CTAs and the dark bands. Warmth is entirely in the blush ground. Taupe is the rollover note, never a fill. The blush pop is rationed — one badge or one feature band per view.

Typography

The serif / sans split is the hierarchy

Buttons & interaction

Black is the action. Taupe is the rollover.

Hover any button — the fill rolls black → taupe. That single move is the entire interaction language: links and focus rings use the same taupe.

Motion · the Silk seed

Calm, 200–300ms, hover ≤1.02

No parallax, no bounce, no scroll-jacking. Gentle section reveals; prefers-reduced-motion is honored. This blush band is the one bold moment on the page — the accent budget the system asks every page to respect, demonstrated on itself.

Imagery & signature motif

Photography carries sections; botanicals are the signature

Warm, intimate, magazine-style photography of real women thriving — diverse, movement and ease, warm ochre/pink grade. Imagery carries sections; it is never replaced by icon rows.

The illustrated botanical (Jordan Price — peonies, irises, lilacs, Newport coastal florals) is the recognizable signature, winning ~10:1 with real customers. Two registers: rich painterly florals for hero/packaging/dividers, fine line-art for quiet contexts. Banned: clip-art, cold/blue botanicals, kawaii, and the engraving/crosshatch style (that's the separate poster system).

Guardrails

The lines that keep it on-brand

Always

  • Warm blush ground everywhere — white is for cards only.
  • Black type and CTAs; taupe only on hover/focus.
  • Exposure at EXPO 0, mixed case, body ≥ 16px.
  • One feature band & one accent pop per page (accent budget).
  • Sticky mobile buy button on every PDP.
  • Restyle third-party widgets to tokens before launch.

Never

  • Cold grays, gradients, glassmorphism, or "tech" elevation.
  • Oxblood as a UI/button color — bottle & photography only.
  • A second loud color, or accent used as a CTA.
  • Blanket UPPERCASE outside tiny eyebrow labels.
  • Anti-aging, woo/mystical, or influencer-cheap framing.
  • Disease claims — structure/function language only.
